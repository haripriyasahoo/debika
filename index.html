Biography

About Debika

I am a Research Scholar at the School of Energy Science and Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Guwahati. My passion lies in harnessing the potential of nanotechnology to solve critical energy and environmental challenges.

Currently, I also serve as a Visiting Researcher at the Department of Materials and Optoelectronic Science, National Sun Yat-sen University, Taiwan. My research primarily focuses on heterostructure charge transfer dynamics, photocatalysis for water pollution mitigation, and the development of novel nanomaterials.

Beyond the lab, I am actively involved in public engagement and leadership, having served as a Postgraduate Senator at IIT Guwahati and organized events for the G20 University Connect.

Academic Work

Current Research Interests

Heterogeneous Photocatalysis

Developing advanced nanomaterials for heterogeneous photocatalysis to efficiently degrade pollutants and generate clean energy under natural sunlight.

Water Pollution Mitigation

Utilizing nanotechnology to address climate change and water pollution. Designing functionalized nanocomposites for the breakdown of organic pollutants in water bodies.

Nanostructure Synthesis

Investigation of charge carrier dynamics in Type I and Type II heterostructures (e.g., ZnO/TiO2, ZnO/Fe3O4) and functionalization of carbon nanotubes (SWCNTs).
